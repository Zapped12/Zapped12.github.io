The founder of FIRST® Robotics
The founder of FIRST Robotics is Dean Kamen, who is an American engineer, inventor, and entrepreneur. Dean Kamen was born in 1951, in Rockville Centre, New York. Kamen is not only known for being the founder of FIRST Robotics, but is also known for inventing the Segway PT and a stair-climbing wheelchair. Kamen has received numerous awards ranging from the National Medal of Technology and Innovation, to the Global Humanitarian Award. Kamen first created FIRST Robotics because he wanted to inspire the youth to become leaders and innovators, as he was disappointed with the number of kids who did not consider science and technology careers.
When FIRST® Robotics was created
FIRST Robotics was founded by Dean Kamen in 1989. The FIRST Robotics headquarters was organized in Manchester, New Hampshire, USA. FIRST Robotics held its first high school competition three years later in 1992, in a high school gymnasium. This began FIRST Robotics' journey of hosting annual international competitions between high schools, to inspire the youth and advertise the beauty of STEM.
The development of FIRST® Robotics
After FIRST Robotics' success in 1992, FIRST Robotics held numerous programs for not only high schoolers, but the younger generations as well, as they hosted tech challenges, and LEGO leagues for elementary and middle school students. Since 1992, FIRST Robotics has grown significantly, with numerous sponsors. FRC has grown from a national event to an international event with numerous divisions.
